## What the Network Actually Looks Like

GTHost runs their own AS (Autonomous System) and IP addresses — they're not reselling bandwidth from a cloud provider. The backbone is built on Juniper Networks equipment with 100GE infrastructure. Bandwidth is unmetered and guaranteed (not "burstable" in the fine print), starting at 300 Mbps and scaling to 10 Gbps.

They publish a Looking Glass portal where you can run ping and traceroute tests to their nodes before ordering — which is exactly the kind of transparency most hosting providers actively avoid. Hardware is Supermicro Blade chassis with Intel Xeon CPUs, Samsung/Micron SSDs, and Seagate HDDs. Enterprise-grade, proven, and — crucially — boring in the best possible way.

The uptime SLA guarantees 100% network availability. If they miss it, compensation is 12× the duration of the outage: five minutes of downtime gets you one hour of credit.

## Who It's Not For

GTHost is unmanaged hosting. You log into clients.gthost.com, you order a server, and then you're in charge of what runs on it. If you're looking for managed WordPress hosting, a drag-and-drop website builder, or someone to update your plugins for you — this isn't that. It's SSH access, full root, and your Linux distro of choice.

The lack of preset "Starter / Pro / Business" tiers is also unusual. Some people find the custom configuration approach refreshing; others find it a little overwhelming at first. The support team is available 24/7 via live chat, email, and phone — so if you're new to configuring dedicated servers, help is reachable.
